When evaluating already-strong Galina drafts (the ones that nail metaphors, "Bottom line:", boiling-frog, numbered prescriptive lists), the residual gap is almost never tone or rhythm. It is two specific things:

1. **First-person operator presence fades after the intro.** Generated drafts use collective "we" well but drop the lived-experience "I" ("In my case," "I've spent years at Siemens/Claroty"). Inject one flag of personal authority near the top that leverages the byline. Caveat: if the flag asserts a fact (e.g. "two decades"), propose the phrasing but flag the number for the user to confirm — do not bake in unverified biographical specifics.

2. **Her signature worldview line is gestured at but not landed.** Drafts paraphrase ("you cannot patch what you have not inventoried") instead of using her actual line "you cannot protect what you cannot see." When the topic touches inventory / third-party / supply-chain / SBOM, land the real phrasing — it is the single highest-leverage voice edit.

**Why:** Observed on a GEO-mode breach-retrospective draft (Change Healthcare / Snowflake / MOVEit, 2026-06-10) that was ~85% match. Everything else was right; these two were the move from "sounds like Galina" to "unmistakably Galina."

**How to apply:** GEO structural features (TL;DR, question subheads, Key takeaways, extractable claims) are intentional — preserve them; do not flag them as un-Galina. "Bottom line:" and question-form subheads actually align with her real habits. Also: "the bad guys" is her idiom; nudge one neutral "the criminals/adversary" toward it, but use sparingly.
